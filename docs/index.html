Deep Learning-Based Prediction of Antimicrobial Resistance from Whole Genome Sequences

Saifelden Mohamed, Salma Hatem

Zewail City of Science and Technology, Giza, Egypt

Abstract

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) poses a critical threat to global public health, necessitating rapid and accurate diagnostic methods. We present a comprehensive deep learning framework for predicting antimicrobial resistance directly from bacterial whole genome sequences. Our approach employs Frequency Chaos Game Representation (FCGR) to transform variable-length genomic sequences into fixed-size numerical representations suitable for convolutional neural network analysis. Using a modified ResNet18 architecture, we evaluated our model across three clinically relevant bacterial species: Staphylococcus aureus, Salmonella enterica, and Escherichia coli. Frequency Chaos Game Representation (FCGR)

FCGR provides an alignment-free method for encoding genomic sequences as numerical matrices. Each genome is represented as a 256×256 matrix where each element corresponds to the normalized frequency of a specific 8-mer. This representation captures both local sequence patterns and global compositional biases while maintaining computational efficiency.

Clinical Implications

Our deep learning approach demonstrates significant clinical potential for rapid AMR screening. The model's high sensitivity ensures minimal false negatives—critical for preventing treatment failures—while maintaining specificity to reduce unnecessary broad-spectrum antibiotic use. Key clinical advantages include:

  • Reduction in diagnostic time from days/weeks to hours
  • Independence from existing resistance gene databases
  • Potential for novel resistance mechanism discovery
  • Scalable deployment across diverse clinical settings

Software Dependencies

Core Libraries: PyTorch, FastAI, Scikit-learn, Pandas, NumPy

Bioinformatics: Sourmash, BioPython, ABRicate

Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn, Plotly

Hardware Requirements

Training: NVIDIA GPU with ≥8GB VRAM

Memory: 16GB RAM minimum, 32GB recommended

Storage: 500GB for full dataset processing

Performance Metrics

Masked Evaluation: Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-score

Advanced Metrics: AUC, MCC, Jaccard Index, Hamming Loss

Cross-Validation: 5-fold GroupKFold with homology awareness
